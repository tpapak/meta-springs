IPD tier-3 spring network — two treatments, random study intercepts AND random treatment effects

Synthetic 2-arm IPD-MA, 50 patients in 5 studies. The model y ~ trt + (trt | study) gives each study its own intercept AND its own treatment-effect, correlated through a 2×2 Σ. This is the Riley/Stewart random-treatment-effect tier-3 IPD-MA model — the one that answers how much the treatment effect varies between studies — rather than sleepstudy's Days slope model.

What the network shows

  • Two types of observation springs by colour: blue (control) pulls only on u_int; red (treated) pulls on BOTH u_int and u_trt (because trt = 1 activates the slope). You can see this in the picture as "two lines per red obs, one line per blue obs".
  • Five red arcs in the ring — the cross-spring per study coupling its random intercept and random treatment-effect. Hardness comes from the off-diagonal of Σ⁻¹.
  • Ten green dashed spokes to the centre — self-shrinkage of every r.e. node toward the pinned zero. Hardnesses are the row sums of Σ⁻¹.
  • β_0 feeds every obs (via u_int); β_trt feeds every treated obs (via u_trt). The yellow bundles show the β→u-node wiring per study.
